Formation of Solara

2.2 Billion Years Ago

Solara, Aeloria's red dwarf, emerged from a dense cosmic cloud. Gravity caused this cloud to condense into a rotating protostellar disk. Over time, nuclear fusion reactions ignited at its core, leading to the birth of Solara. Initially, it emitted brilliant light, but it eventually evolved into a stable red dwarf, providing Aeloria with consistent illumination.
